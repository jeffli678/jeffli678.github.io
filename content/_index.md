## About

I am a software developer at [Vector 35 Inc.](https://vector35.com), where I am one of the core developers of [Binary Ninja](https://binary.ninja/) and lead the development of its [debugger](https://github.com/Vector35/debugger), including its [Time-Travel Debugging (TTD) integration](https://docs.binary.ninja/guide/debugger/dbgeng-ttd.html). My research interests span reverse engineering, malware analysis, vulnerability research, and code obfuscation. I am also an independent security researcher with multiple CVEs to my name ([CVE-2025-13453](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2025-13453), [CVE-2025-13454](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2025-13454), [CVE-2025-13455](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2025-13455)).

I have spoken at [RE//verse](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rv6jdnQ4YhY) (2026) and [CODEGATE](https://codegate.org/) (2025), guested on the [*Behind the Binary*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=50QiuaJ6l8M&list=PLjiTz6DAEpuLAykjYGpAUDL-tCrmTpXTf) podcast by Google Cloud Security, and have been invited to present on various technical platforms. I have published in peer-reviewed journals including the *Journal of Computer Security* and *IEEE TIFS*, co-authored a book on assembly language and reverse engineering, and created an introductory [Binary Ninja course on Open Security Training 2 (OST2)](https://ost2.fyi/Dbg1103). I am a peer reviewer for CCS, ESORICS, DSN, JCS, and other cybersecurity venues. I am a reviewer of [*Paged Out!*](https://pagedout.institute/) magazine, and the administrator and reviewer of [crackmes.one](https://crackmes.one). I took [1st place in the Grand Reverse Engineering Challenge](https://binary.ninja/2021/09/02/winning-the-grand-re-challenge.html), completed the [Flare-On](https://flare-on.com/) reverse engineering challenge four consecutive times (2022–2025), and am a member of the [Shellphish](https://shellphish.net/) CTF team. I also contribute to widely-used open-source security tooling, including Google FLARE's [capa](https://github.com/mandiant/capa) (120k+ annual downloads) and [CodeLLDB](https://github.com/vadimcn/codelldb) (8.4M+ installs).
